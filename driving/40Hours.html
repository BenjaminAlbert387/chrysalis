Nerves

Another week, another driving lesson.
I did another mock test and the nerves got to me so much.
I almost got too close to parked cars on the left (because there were oncoming cars and I couldn’t tell if I had enough space).
But I realised and adjusted my position. Then parallel parking, I accidentally went full off too soon.
I did it perfectly twice right after, I knew what to do I just fumbled it.
Apart from those two problems, everything was fine otherwise.
Forward bay parking, done smoothly.
Reverse parking, one shot and done. (I was basically grinning with myself because I’d never thought I’d see the day.)
But let’s not get too cocky and overconfident.
Pull up on the right, sorted and with good observations too.
We practiced emergency stop, bonnet questions and all the auxiliary controls.
The only other stupid (and I mean very stupid) mistake I did was thinking I was on a mini roundabout.
When I was actually at a junction and therefore tried to turn right.
With a car oncoming. Not my finest moment.
After that though, no other issues on the drive.
All I gotta do for my safe driving assessment is drive legally, safely and confidently.
I’ve written down driving affirmations, study “the blade” aka YouTube videos, and not do something dumb.
It’s weird how I’ve said congratulations to so many people, all the advice I’ve received and given on the subreddit.
Every time I got downvoted to hell and definitely did not take it personally. Oops.
Whatever, I think I’m waffling. But it’s not over yet.
As for when it’s actually booked…you won’t know until it happens!
I haven’t told anyone, not even my parents.
